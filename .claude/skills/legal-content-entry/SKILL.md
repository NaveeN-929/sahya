# Legal / Knowledge-Platform Content Entry

This is Sahay's highest content-liability surface after crisis detection (PRD §9.2, §15.2).
Content here is never freely generated by the model at request time — it's written,
reviewed, and stored, then retrieved via RAG (PRD §9.2.1). This skill governs writing or
editing an entry, not the retrieval mechanism (see `new-ai-agent` for that).

## Before writing anything

- Identify which PRD §3 taxonomy category this content falls under, and check its current
  risk/readiness flag:
  - **Comparatively low-risk, clear law:** cyber abuse (§3.8), elder abuse (§3.9) — these
    ship first per MVP scope (PRD §16.1).
  - **High legal-accuracy risk, requires Tier 1 legal review before publish:** custody/
    parental access (§3.4), sexual abuse/coercion (§3.7) — do not draft these as if
    publishable without an explicit reviewer sign-off recorded.
  - Everything else sits in between — assume review is required unless told otherwise.

## Hard content rules (PRD §9.2, §15.2, §15.4)

- **Explain process, never assess merits.** "Here's what anticipatory bail generally is and
  when courts typically consider it" — yes. "Based on what you've described, you'd likely
  qualify" — never, in this content or in any agent prompt referencing it.
- **No outcome prediction, no suggested wording for a filing/complaint/FIR response, no
  specific legal strategy.** Anything that reads as advice to a specific situation belongs
  in the lawyer-referral flow, not this knowledge base.
- **No advocacy framing.** No content asserting or implying allegations against men are
  generally false as a class; no aggregated "misuse" statistics; no framing existing
  protections for women (PWDVA, BNS cruelty provisions, POSH Act) as themselves the problem.
  State law and data; don't take a position on what the law should become.
- **Cite the source.** Every factual/statutory claim needs a named, specific citation (a
  numbered provision, a named act, a named study) — never "it is generally understood that."
  Reference PRD §2.4's NCRB-data discipline as the model: precise, source-traceable, no
  popularized-but-debunked figures.
- **Post-BNS/BNSS/BSA accuracy.** India's criminal procedure transitioned from
  IPC/CrPC/Evidence Act to BNS/BNSS/BSA (2023) — confirm any statute reference uses current
  nomenclature and confirm the underlying provision actually changed/didn't change in the
  transition before citing it.
- **Never imply court-readiness.** Don't write or approve copy suggesting uploaded evidence
  or generated content is "court-ready" or automatically admissible (PRD §15.1) — integrity
  preservation (hash verification) is a real but partial contribution, not a certificate.

## Required metadata on every entry (`knowledge_chunks` row, PRD §10.3)

- `content_category` — maps to PRD §3 taxonomy.
- `source_citation` — the specific statute/study/professional source.
- `reviewed_by`, `reviewed_at` — must be populated with a real reviewer before the entry is
  eligible for retrieval by the Legal Information Agent. An entry without these is draft-only
  and must not be wired into the live RAG index.
- Display requirement on the frontend (FR-2.2): every legal-content article shows its review
  status, last-reviewed date, and source citations — don't ship a content surface that hides
  this.

## Workflow

1. Draft the content against an identified PRD §3 category and the rules above.
2. Mark it explicitly unreviewed/draft until a named legal reviewer signs off (PRD §7.1
   Tier 1) — don't insert into the production `knowledge_chunks` table pre-review.
3. On approval, record `reviewed_by`/`reviewed_at`, embed, and insert.
4. Confirm the article surface renders review status/date/citations per FR-2.2 before
   considering this done.
